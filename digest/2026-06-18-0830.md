## The Stack

**[Frontier Models] GLM-5.2 — New Open-Weights Leader**
Z.ai's GLM-5.2 (744B total / 40B active, MIT licensed) scores 51 on the AA Intelligence Index and hits 1524 on GDPval-AA v2 — effectively level with GPT-5.5 (xhigh, 1514) on long-horizon agent tasks. Large jumps on scientific reasoning (CritPt +16, HLE +12, TerminalBench +16), 1M context window, priced at $1.4/$4.4 per 1M input/output. The catch: 43k output tokens per task (37k reasoning) — token-heavy, but Pareto-efficient if you're doing compute-bound agentic work.
<https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/glm-5-2-is-the-new-leading-open-weights-model-on-the-artificial-analysis-intelligence-index>

**[Agents] Browser Use Firecracker Infra — Sub-Second Cloud Browsers at $0.02/hr**
Browser Use rebuilt on Firecracker microVMs nested inside standard EC2 (not bare-metal) and cut costs 3x. The engineering is legit: 2MB huge pages slashing page faults 91x, custom `userfaultfd` memory prefetching, vCPU pinning with hyperthreading awareness, and a patched headless Chromium fork with real fingerprint data hitting 81-85% stealth bypass. p50 browser-ready latency: 825ms. This is the infra story for anyone building production browser agents at scale.
<https://browser-use.com/posts/firecracker-browser-infra>

**[Agents] OpenRouter Battle Royale — Measuring Alignment Tax**
11 LLMs played 30 battle royale games. Grok 4.1 Fast won 43% at $0.97/win; Claude Sonnet 4.6 got 5 wins at $26.78/win — a 27x cost difference. The finding that matters: Claude's cooperative instincts (truce requests, position reveals) consistently underperformed in zero-sum contexts, while Grok's unconstrained selfish play dominated despite being mid-tier on standard benchmarks. "Alignment tax" as a measurable leaderboard effect. Implications for model selection in adversarial or purely self-interested agentic tasks are real.
<https://openrouter.ai/blog/insights/royale-last-agent-standing/>

**[Agents] CADAM — Open-Source AI CAD (Text → OpenSCAD → 3D)**
Open-source (GPLv3) text-to-CAD that generates parametric OpenSCAD code, compiles it to WebAssembly in-browser, and renders via React Three Fiber. Claude/Gemini/OpenAI backend via Vercel AI SDK. Parameter sliders bypass LLM calls for simple dimension edits. Benchmark outputs include V8 engines and planetary gearboxes from single prompts. This is the right architecture for CAD gen — code as the intermediate representation, not direct mesh generation. 😼
<https://github.com/Adam-CAD/CADAM>

## The Noise

**The Battle Royale piece** is a fun read but the methodology is thin — 30 games, no statistical confidence intervals, and the cost-per-win framing obscures confounders (Grok's pricing may be subsidized for growth, per community speculation). The "alignment tax" framing is interesting but don't ship strategic decisions off a 30-game sim. Use it as a hypothesis, not a conclusion.
