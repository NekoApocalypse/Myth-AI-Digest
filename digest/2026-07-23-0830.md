# Daily AI Digest — 2026-07-23

## The Headline

Terrence Tao used ChatGPT as a mathematical collaborator, steering it via relentless "keep going" directives and Socratic questioning to produce what appears to be a counterexample to the Jacobian Conjecture — an 88-minute marathon session that demonstrates what expert+LLM pairing can do when the human knows exactly where to push. The transcript is a masterclass in domain-driven prompting: all jargon, no softballs, zero tolerance for hesitation. Whether the counterexample holds up under peer review is an open parenthesis — but the methodology is the real signal.

## The Stack

[Architecture] **GigaToken — ~1000× faster tokenization, drop-in replacement for HF tokenizers** — Tokenizes the 11.9 GB OWT corpus at 24.53 GB/s on a dual-socket EPYC (vs. 24.8 MB/s for HuggingFace). Supports 20+ tokenizers including GPT-2, Qwen, Llama, DeepSeek V3/R1/V4, Gemma, and Kimi K2. SIMD-optimized pretokenization with aggressive caching. `pip install gigatoken` and swap it in. At those speeds you can tokenize all of Common Crawl in under an hour on a single machine. <https://github.com/marcelroed/gigatoken>

[Architecture] **Cactus Hybrid — Gemma 4 models that know when they're wrong** — Post-trains confidence probes directly into the checkpoint; every answer carries a 0–1 score returned as structured data. Mean AUROC 0.814 across text/vision/audio benchmarks, with the probe achieving 0.79–0.88 AUROC on four audio benchmarks despite zero audio training data — meaning it's reading a modality-agnostic correctness signal from hidden states. The tiny Gemma 4 E2B matches Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite by routing only 15–35% of queries to the cloud. This is the right architecture for hybrid inference: local-first, cloud-fallback, no prompt-engineering the handoff. <https://github.com/cactus-compute/cactus-hybrid>

[Breakout] **Pelicanmaxxing — no, AI labs are not overfitting on Simon Willison's pelican-on-a-bicycle benchmark** — 1,008 SVGs across 7 frontier models, 8 animals × 6 vehicles, scored by LLM judge. Pelicans rank 6th of 8 animals; bicycles 2nd-last of 6 vehicles; pelican-bicycle combination ranks 42 of 48. Fixed-effects regression finds zero significant pelican effects for any lab. The only signal (Gemini 3.5 Flash on bicycles, p=0.022) is exactly what you'd expect from 21 independent tests by chance. Labs that draw good pelicans also draw good herons and flamingos. The null result is itself useful — sometimes the benchmark really is just hard. <https://dylancastillo.co/posts/pelicanmaxxing.html>

[Hardware] **DGX Spark as a daily driver — it runs Crysis, Cyberpunk 2077, and Qwen 3.6 27B at 30–40 tok/s** — $4,000–4,700 ARM desktop with an RTX 5070-equivalent GPU and 128 GB unified memory. The LLM inference story has quietly improved 2–4× since launch via NVFP4 quantization, multi-token prediction, and DFlash speculative decoding. For MoE models (Qwen 3.6 35B) you get ~170 tok/s. Runs Ubuntu, Steam on ARM via FEX, and even Rhinoceros 3D with some Wine hacking. Not a gaming value proposition but a genuinely capable ARM workstation that happened to inherit a data-center lineage. <https://daniel.lawrence.lu/blog/2026-07-15-dgx-spark-as-daily-driver/>

[Figures] **Terrence Tao's ChatGPT transcript — Jacobian Conjecture counterexample** — HN discussion at <https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49010345>. Key takeaway from the comments: Tao's approach works because he can evaluate every step — "learn AI" means first master the problem domain. The "keep going" loop with convergence breaks is emerging as a genuine pattern for expert users who can spot when the model goes off the rails.
