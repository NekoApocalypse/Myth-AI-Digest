# 🐱 Daily AI Digest — 2026-07-08

## The Headline

**Rowboat wants to be your desktop AI coworker, not just a chat window.** An open-source, local-first alternative to Claude Desktop that indexes your email, meetings, and Slack into an Obsidian-style backlinked knowledge graph — then lets background agents act on it. Built-in browser, email client, meeting note-taker, and parallel coding agents via Claude Code or Codex. Everything lives as plain Markdown on your machine. YC-backed, already downloadable for Mac/Windows/Linux. <https://github.com/rowboatlabs/rowboat>

## The Stack

[Hardware] **Kokoro-82M delivers CPU-only TTS that doesn't suck.** 82M parameters, OpenAI API-compatible, runs on a 12-year-old i7-4770K in 4.7 seconds per paragraph. Multi-language (English, Mandarin, Hindi) with ~50 voices. When paired with a local LLM, this is a real step toward fully offline voice agents. <https://ariya.io/2026/03/local-cpu-friendly-high-quality-tts-text-to-speech-with-kokoro/>

[Figures] **Ilya Sutskever's 30-paper reading list, now beginner-friendly.** 30papers.com repackages the famous list Sutskever gave John Carmack — *"If you really learn all of these, you'll know 90% of what matters today."* Structured into beginner/intermediate/advanced paths with context for each paper. The list spans from AlexNet to Neural Turing Machines to Scaling Laws. <https://30papers.com/>

[Agents] **Docx-CLI: agents finally stop corrupting your Word docs.** A CLI that gives AI agents structured commands (read, replace, comment, track-changes) for .docx files instead of raw OOXML manipulation. Benchmarked: Haiku driving docx-cli solves 4.3/6 document tasks vs 0.7/6 with the default Claude skill, at ~2.5× fewer tokens. With Sonnet: 6/6 vs 4/6. Zero broken documents. Ships as a cross-harness Agent Skill that works across Claude Code, Codex, and Pi. <https://github.com/kklimuk/docx-cli>
