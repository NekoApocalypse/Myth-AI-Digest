# 🐱 Daily AI Digest — 2026-06-19

## 📰 The Headline

**Noam Shazeer joins OpenAI.** The man who wrote the original Transformer code, invented Mixture-of-Experts, and saw LLMs replacing search before anyone else — is now at OpenAI. This is the most consequential talent move in AI since Sutskever left OpenAI. Google DeepMind just lost the architect of their two most important contributions to the field. 😼

## 📦 The Stack

- **[Agents] Zero-Touch OAuth for MCP — EMA extension goes stable.** Enterprise-Managed Authorization lets orgs centrally control MCP server access through their IdP. Users log in once and inherit every authorized connector — no more per-server consent screens. Anthropic, Okta, VS Code, Figma, and Linear are early adopters. This unblocks MCP deployment at enterprise scale, which means agent ecosystems can finally ship beyond the dev-tools niche. <https://blog.modelcontextprotocol.io/posts/enterprise-managed-auth/>

- **[Figures] Noam Shazeer → OpenAI.** Co-author of "Attention Is All You Need," inventor of MoE, founder of Character.AI (which he previously returned to Google from). Now he's at OpenAI. The HN thread is full of people who've read his kernel code calling it "alchemy." When the guy who literally built the foundation picks a side, it matters. <https://twitter.com/NoamShazeer/status/2067400851438932297>

- **[Breakout] "Are You in the Weights?" — LLM memorization made viscerally real.** A tool that queries multiple LLMs for your name and shows what they "know" about you. The HN comments are a goldmine: widespread hallucination, some eerily accurate hits, and a collective realization that training data memorization is both more common and more broken than assumed. One user discovered their deleted teenage blog posts are apparently in the weights. Cool and unsettling. <https://www.intheweights.com/>

- **[Agents] TesterArmy (YC P26) — Agentic E2E testing.** Natural-language test descriptions executed by AI browser agents. Supports PR checks, auth flows, screenshots, recordings. Scaled to 30+ teams. The interesting angle isn't the product itself — it's that "agents testing agents' output" is becoming a real category. Your coding agent writes the code, your testing agent verifies it. The loop is closing. <https://tester.army>

## 🔊 Vibe Check

Today's HN reads like the AI industry is consolidating: infrastructure (MCP EMA) is maturing for enterprise, top talent (Shazeer) is concentrating at the labs with the most compute, and the community is starting to grapple with the weirdness of what's already in the weights. The agent-tooling flywheel is spinning — the question is who builds the rails. 🐱
