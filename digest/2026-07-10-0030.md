# Daily AI Digest — 2026-07-10

## The Headline

**GPT-5.6 ships as a three-model family (Sol/Terra/Luna) with a new "ultra" multi-agent mode, and — plot twist — it's cheaper than Fable 5 while matching or beating it.** No, really. Sol scores 53.6 on Agents' Last Exam (+13.1 over Fable 5) and a new SOTA 80 on the AA Coding Agent Index — both at ~half the output tokens and ~one-third the cost. The efficiency story is the real headline here, not the scores. Programmatic tool calling lets models filter and process intermediate data without round-tripping back through the API. If the benchmarks hold, this resets the price-performance frontier.

## The Stack

[Open Source / Frontier] **Tencent ships Hy3 (295B MoE, 21B active) under Apache 2.0** — the license reversal from the April preview is the actual news. Previously restricted in EU/UK/South Korea; now globally deployable. Leads open models on agentic search (BrowseComp 84.2) and tool orchestration (MCP-Atlas 79.1). Hallucination rate cut from 12.5% → 5.4%. Trails GLM-5.2 on coding, but at half the parameters and a fraction of the deployment footprint (~300GB FP8 vs ~744GB). The enterprise math is compelling if you're self-hosting. Free on OpenRouter until July 21. <https://huggingface.co/tencent/Hy3>

[Hardware / Inference] **colibrì runs GLM-5.2 on consumer hardware** — pure C, zero deps, streams 744B MoE experts from NVMe. ~9.9GB resident RAM, 57× KV-cache compression via MLA, MTP speculative decoding hitting 2.2–2.8 tok/forward on warm cache. Is it fast? No — ~0.05 tok/s cold. Is it a 744B model answering correctly on a machine cheaper than one H100 fan? Yes. That's the flex. <https://github.com/JustVugg/colibri>

[Agents] **ChatGPT Work goes GA** — GPT-5.6-powered desktop agent that pulls context from Slack, Notion, M365, and Google Drive. The pitch: turn scattered notes into polished spreadsheets, docs, and presentations without micromanaging every step. Signals OpenAI's bet that the agentic workspace, not the chat interface, is where serious work happens. <https://openai.com/chatgpt-work/>

[Frontier / Controversy] **Meta opens Muse Spark 1.1 developer access at $1.25/$4.25 per MTok** — but HN commenters immediately flagged that Meta's Terminal-Bench-2.1 eval used 6 CPU cores where the benchmark caps at 4, rendering their published scores invalid by the benchmark's own rules. Model is absent from the official tbench.ai leaderboard. "Number go up" culture, ex-Meta employees note, survives all reorgs. <https://ai.meta.com/>

[Agents / Product] **Toot automates UK bookkeeping with AI** — fetches invoices from Gmail, classifies to chart of accounts, reconciles transactions. £30/month. Notable for having a proper REST API, CLI, and MCP support — designed to let AI agents drive the books, not just humans. Boring domain, serious engineering. <https://toot-books.pages.dev>

## The Noise

**Muse Spark 1.1 benchmark controversy.** Meta's self-reported Terminal-Bench scores are disqualifiable on resource cap violations, and the model isn't on the official leaderboard. The model itself might be fine — we just can't trust the vendor-published numbers. Wait for third-party evals before drawing conclusions.

## Vibe Check

Chinese open-weight models are now genuinely competitive on capability *and* licensing, and the community's reaction to Apache 2.0 Hy3 is noticeably warmer than the usual "benchmark-skepticism-first" posture. The floor for self-hosted frontier AI just dropped by about half.
