## The Stack

- **[Architecture] Un-0: Generating Images with Coupled Oscillators** — Reads out images by evolving thousands of coupled oscillators through time under trained coupling weights. The learnable parameters are the coupling matrix K and natural frequencies ω — no feedforward layers. Code, weights, and ablation studies all open at <https://unconv.ai/blog/introducing-un-0-generating-images-with-coupled-oscillators/>.

- **[Hardware] IBM debuts sub-1nm "nanostack" chip architecture** — 3D sequential integration stacks transistors vertically, packing ~100B transistors on a fingernail. Validated at VLSI 2026 with functional CMOS inverters and 40% SRAM scaling. Yes, the "0.7nm" label is marketing — features are ~5nm — but the 3D stacking concept itself is a real architectural shift, not just another node shrink. <https://newsroom.ibm.com/2026-06-25-ibm-debuts-worlds-first-sub-1-nanometer-chip-technology>

- **[Agents] OpenKnowledge — open-source AI-native markdown editor** — WYSIWYG editor with first-class Claude, Codex, and Cursor integration via MCP/CLI. CRDT + git for agent-aware collaborative editing, built-in RAG for LLM wikis. The bidirectional ProseMirror↔markdown pipeline (lossless round-trip through ASTs) and dual-observer CRDT are the interesting bits. <https://github.com/inkeep/open-knowledge>
