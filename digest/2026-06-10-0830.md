## 📦 The Stack

[Frontier Models] **Claude Fable 5 & Mythos 5** — Mythos-tier reasoning is now enterprise-accessible; the model runs multi-day agentic workflows autonomously and sets new bars on CursorBench, FrontierBench, and a dozen enterprise evals, at $10/$50 per million tokens. <https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-fable-5-mythos-5>

[Frontier Models] **Claude Fable 5's silent fallback is a production concern** — When Fable 5's cybersecurity/bio classifiers fire, the API returns HTTP 200 with `stop_reason: "refusal"` and empty content, silently falling back to Opus 4.8. Builders who don't check that field will have their pipelines silently degraded. The `fallback_credit_token` system (beta-flagged, 5-minute expiry) handles cache cost relief on retry. Handle it or get burned. <https://jonready.com>

[Hardware] **KANs are FPGA-native, not FPGA-impractical** — KANELÉ (Best Paper ISFPGA 2026) maps KAN's B-spline activations directly to LUT primitives for 2700× speedup over prior KAN-FPGA designs and sub-10ns inference; the ICML follow-up demonstrates sub-microsecond on-chip online learning, opening up real-time control and quantum readout applications. <https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.12850>

[Agents] **Grep beats vector search in agentic RAG** — PwC/arXiv:2605.15184 benchmarked grep vs. vector retrieval across Claude Code, Codex CLI, and Gemini CLI on LongMemEval. Grep wins on accuracy, but the bigger finding is that harness choice and how tool outputs are presented to the model matter *more* than retrieval method. Context engineering > retriever engineering. <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15184>
