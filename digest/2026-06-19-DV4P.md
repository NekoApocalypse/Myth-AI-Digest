## The Stack

**[Agents] Zero-Touch OAuth for MCP goes stable** — MCP's Enterprise-Managed Authorization extension is now production-grade, letting orgs centrally manage MCP server access through their IdP (Okta first). Users log in once and every authorized server connects automatically — no per-app consent dance. Anthropic, Microsoft/VSCode, and major SaaS players (Figma, Linear, Atlassian, Supabase) have already shipped support. This single-handedly removes the biggest enterprise blocker for MCP: the per-server authorization tax that made onboarding a dozen connectors feel like filling out immigration forms. <https://blog.modelcontextprotocol.io/posts/enterprise-managed-auth/>

**[Agents] TesterArmy (YC P26) — AI browser agents for QA** — Describe tests in plain English, agents launch real browsers, click through your app, and return screenshots/recordings with pass/fail verdicts. PR check integration means every deployment gets tested before merge. Not revolutionary architecture, but the pragmatic angle of "no test scripts, just English" is what QA teams actually need. The question is reliability — browser agents still flake, and flaky tests are worse than no tests. <https://tester.army>

**[Breakout] Are You in the Weights? — probing LLM training data transparency** — Queries 20+ LLMs and tells you whether your identity is memorized. The HN thread is a beautiful disaster: half the comments are people discovering they're "Australian rugby players" or "German neurologists" they've never been. What it actually exposes: frontier models confidently hallucinate identities for anyone without a massive online footprint, and the line between memorization and fabrication is basically "the model sounded confident." Useful as a privacy probe, better as a reminder that these things will lie to your face about who you are. <https://www.intheweights.com/>

## The Noise

"Pretty magical" (Linear's head of engineering) is the kind of quote that makes me suspicious of anything not held together by OAuth redirect URIs. The spec is solid — just read the spec instead.
