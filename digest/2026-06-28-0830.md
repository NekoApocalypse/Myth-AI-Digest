## The Stack

**[Architecture / Hardware] DSpark: Speculative Decoding Training Framework**
DeepSeek released a complete codebase for training and evaluating draft models for speculative decoding. Includes data prep, training configs, and eval across GSM8K, MATH500, AIME25, HumanEval, MBPP, and LiveCodeBench. Default configs assume 8 GPUs and ~38TB of target cache. The real signal: three distinct algorithms (DSpark, DFlash, Eagle3) in one repo, with the DSpark paper showing a practical spec-decoding approach that doesn't require exotic hardware.
<https://github.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSpec>

**[Agents] Adrafinil: Lid-Closed Mac Awake Only While Agents Work**
A clever macOS utility that keeps a Mac awake through lid-close *only* when AI coding agents (Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, Gemini CLI, Aider, Hermes, OpenCode, Cline, Pi) have active sessions. Architecture is clean: reference-counted assertions in a user daemon, root-level helper for clamshell sleep via `pmset`, thermal cutout to prevent cooking your laptop in a bag, and a sub-50ms CLI for agent hook integration. This is the kind of agent-infra tooling that actually matters — solves a real friction point without over-engineering.
<https://github.com/kageroumado/adrafinil>

**[Frontier Models] Asian AI Startups Fill the Mythos Void**
Sakana AI (Tokyo) launched Fugu, an orchestration model that coordinates access to other models through APIs — explicitly positioned as a hedge against US export controls. China's 360 unveiled Tulongfeng (vulnerability discovery) and Yitianzhen (automated defense). Both launches are direct reactions to the US government blocking Mythos/Fable 5 for non-US citizens. Sakana frames it as "collective intelligence" rather than model monoculture; 360 calls it a national strategic asset.
<https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/27/asian-ai-startups-launch-mythos-like-models-as-anthropics-export-ban-drags-on/>

**[Breakout] Post-Mythos Cybersecurity: Keep Calm and Carry On**
A sober technical analysis of Mythos's actual cybersecurity impact. Key takeaways: (1) the $20K-per-bug BSD finding was the result of a thousand scaffold runs, not a single prompt — the harness matters; (2) smaller models like Gemma 4 and Qwen 3.6 find ~half the vulnerabilities Mythos does when given the same scaffolding; (3) the real differentiator is exploit proof-of-capability, not just detection — this eliminates the false-positive flood that plagued earlier AI bug hunters; (4) OpenAI is entering the same space with GPT-5.6 Sol/Terra/Luna, biased toward defense. The article's core argument: Mythos is a gradual improvement, not a paradigm shift, and basic defense-in-depth still works.
<https://cephalosec.com/blog/cybersecurity-in-the-post-mythos-era-keep-calm-and-carry-on/>
