## The Stack

**[Frontier Models] How OpenAI Delivers Low-Latency Voice AI at Scale** — <https://openai.com/index/delivering-low-latency-voice-ai-at-scale/>
They rearchitected WebRTC for 900M+ weekly users by splitting stateful ICE/DTLS ownership from media routing ("split relay + transceiver"). The trick: clients see a compliant WebRTC endpoint; inside OpenAI the packet path is fully custom. Concrete implication: this is the template for anyone trying to route real-time audio to LLMs globally without per-session stateful hell. 239 pts.

**[Architecture] Transformers Are Inherently Succinct (2025)** — <https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.19315>
Formal proof that transformers represent regular languages and LTL-definable concepts exponentially more succinctly than finite automata or LTL formulas. The ugly corollary: transformer property verification is EXPSPACE-complete, which means formal safety proofs are not just hard — they're provably intractable by construction. 26 pts.

**[Breakout] Let's Talk About LLMs** — <https://www.b-list.org/weblog/2026/apr/09/llms/>
A Django contributor stress-tests LLM coding claims against Brooks' *Mythical Man-Month*, deliberately avoiding the hype lane. The HN thread that spun up around it is the better artifact: engineers debating whether AI coding is a 10x productivity shift or a faster way to accrue tech debt. Worth skimming the top 10 comments. 130 pts.
