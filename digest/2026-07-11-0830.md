## The Stack

**[Breakout] Boko Haram Uses Frontier AI — Cambridge CASP Report** — Semi-structured interviews with 27 former Boko Haram members reveal institutionalized AI use across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Meta AI, and DeepSeek for attack planning, bomb design, and operational logistics. Users successfully circumvented safety guardrails. The research is peer-reviewed, field-validated, and published by the University of Cambridge — not a think-tank hot take. The paper puts a hard timestamp on "terrorist groups are already operationalizing frontier models." <https://casp.ac/reports/ai-enabled-terrorism>

**[Architecture / Hardware] MiMo v2.5 Full-Pipeline Inference Optimization** — Xiaomi's engineering team published a detailed breakdown of bringing Hybrid Sliding Window Attention (SWA) to production. The core win: KVCache compression to ~1/7 of Full Attention via a dual-pool design (separate Full KV and SWA KV pools), SWA-aware prefix cache trees with "window-safe length" matching, and layerwise KVCache prefetch for near-zero cache-read overhead. Solid engineering porn. Low points (30) but high technical density — the kind of post that ages well. <https://mimo.xiaomi.com/blog/mimo-v2-5-inference>

**[Agents] Frugon — Local LLM Cost Analyzer** — Open-source tool that reads your JSONL call logs, prices them against a basket of models, and tells you which percentage of calls can route to cheaper models at a given quality tolerance. Example from the demo: 56,100 calls baseline $549/mo on GPT-5.5 → $344/mo by routing 64% of calls to DeepSeek V4 Flash, a 37% savings. Fully local, no telemetry, MIT licensed. Not AI — but directly useful to people building on AI. <https://github.com/Rodiun/frugon>
