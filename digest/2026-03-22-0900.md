# Daily AI Digest — 2026-03-22

## The Headline
**Mamba-3 beats a 1.5B Transformer on prefill+decode latency at every sequence length** — inference-first SSM design is arriving just as RLVR and agentic workloads are making inference the actual bottleneck.

---

## The Stack

**[Architecture]** **Mamba-3** — CMU/Princeton/Cartesia/Together AI redesigned the SSM from the ground up for inference efficiency: richer complex-valued recurrence, MIMO SSM variants, no short conv, and RoPE — benchmarks show it beats Mamba-2, Gated DeltaNet, *and* Llama-3.2-1B on combined prefill+decode latency at 1.5B scale. Kernels open-sourced in Triton/TileLang/CuTe. This is the right bet for a world where post-training and agentic inference dominate compute.

**[Hardware]** **Tinybox (red v2 / green v2)** — tinygrad's compute hardware now ships commercially: 4×9070XT at $12k (778 TFLOPS FP16, 64GB VRAM) or 4×RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell at $65k (3086 TFLOPS, 384GB VRAM). The Exabox (1 EFLOP, 720 RDNA5 GPUs, ~$10M) is in the pipeline for 2027. Practically the only off-the-shelf option for running 120B+ models fully local at sane BW, with tinygrad as the native stack.

**[Frontier Models]** **Meta Omnilingual MT (OMT)** — first MT system supporting 1,600+ languages, covering more than 8× prior NLLB coverage. Achieved via LLaMA-based decoder-only (OMT-LLaMA) and encoder-decoder (OMT-NLLB) architectures; 1B–8B specialists match or beat a 70B LLM baseline — strong specialization signal. BOUQuET benchmark and MeDLEY bitext datasets released publicly. The "long tail of languages" problem just got a lot smaller.
