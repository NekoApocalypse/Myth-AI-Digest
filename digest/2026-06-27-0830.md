## 4. Open vs. Closed LLMs — A Quantitative Gap Analysis

**151pts · 159 comments** · [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48692058) · [Doubleword Blog](https://blog.doubleword.ai/frontier-os-llm)

A rigorous quantitative analysis tracking the gap between open-weight and closed-source LLMs using the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index. Key findings:

- **The trend is real but uneven.** On the headline AA Intelligence Index, the gap has been shrinking since summer 2024, with a best-fit line hitting zero around December 3rd, 2026.
- **But it depends entirely on which benchmark you look at.** Across 18 different benchmarks, the average gap is flat at ~5 months. The headline convergence is driven almost entirely by **coding benchmarks**, where open models went from 15 months behind to 1-2 months. Most other categories show moderate *increasing* gaps.
- **Measurement is hard.** Depending on your choice of metric, you either predict open-source singularity by Christmas, or conclude open-source is consistently 5 months behind with the gap possibly widening.

**Bottom line:** Open-source is catching up fast where it matters most (coding), but claims of imminent parity with frontier models need to specify *which* frontier on *which* benchmark. The story is more nuanced than a single trendline.

## 5. Show HN: Smart Model Routing for Claude Code, Codex, and Cursor

**136pts · 86 comments** · [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48688700) · [GitHub](https://github.com/workweave/router)

Workweave open-sourced a model router that plugs into coding agents (Claude Code, Codex, Cursor) and routes each request to the best model. Built as an Anthropic/OpenAI-compatible proxy endpoint.

**How it works:**
- Trained an RL model on tens of thousands of agent traces, rewarding the router when the selected LLM completes the task
- Planning tasks → frontier models (Opus 4.8)
- Codebase exploration → cheaper models (DeepSeek V4 Flash)
- Implementation → mid-tier models (GLM 5.2, Kimi K2.6)

**Results:** 40% token savings vs. baseline with no noticeable quality drop. Source-available under Elastic License 2.0.

**Routing tech:** Based on Avengers-Pro (arxiv 2508.12631), a test-time routing framework that ensembles LLMs by embedding incoming queries and routing based on a performance-efficiency score. Accepted to DAI 2025.

**Bottom line:** The model router pattern is going mainstream. If you're spending real money on coding agents, this is a 40% cost cut with one endpoint change. `npx @workweave/router` and you're done.

## 6. AI in Mathematics Is Forcing Big Questions

**187pts · 85 comments** · [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48692883) · [IEEE Spectrum](https://spectrum.ieee.org/ai-in-mathematics)

IEEE Spectrum examines how AI is changing the nature of mathematical work. Terence Tao envisions "Big Mathematics" — humans and machines collaborating on problems at a scale impossible for either alone.

The piece traces a thoughtful arc: from the four-color theorem (computer-assisted proof, 1976) to today's AI systems that can propose conjectures and generate proofs. The fundamental tension isn't whether AI can do math — it's whether the *struggle* that mathematicians find meaningful will remain relevant.

Jeremy Avigad (CMU): "It's just a wonderful feeling when you've been thinking long and hard about something complex, difficult, and then — all of a sudden — it just comes together." The question is whether AI short-circuits that process, or amplifies it.

**Bottom line:** Not a technical paper, but a well-framed reflection on what happens when AI enters a field where the *process* of discovery is inseparable from its value. Worth reading for anyone thinking about AI's impact on creative/intellectual work.

## 8. Data Centers Trigger Voter Backlash — Politicians Losing Elections

**154pts · 277 comments** · [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48689275) · [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/cost-me-the-election-data-centers-trigger-voter-backlash-12118327)

The AI infrastructure boom is hitting political reality. Key developments:

- **Utah Senate President J. Stuart Adams lost his primary** after backing the Stratos project — a data center campus requiring 9 GW (more than Utah's entire current electricity usage). Multiple county commissioners who voted for it also lost.
- **57% of Americans oppose data centers in their community** (Reuters/Ipsos, June 2026).
- **Energy prices are "the current face of affordability"** — data centers make a simple and compelling villain story when voters are already skeptical of AI.
- **NDAs and secrecy** are backfiring. Politicians are signing NDAs that prevent them from telling voters what they've agreed to, which fuels backlash.
- **Bipartisan problem.** Neither party owns this — Democrats face pressure from the left on environmental concerns, Republicans face voters who feel the costs personally.

HN discussion surfaces the externality problem: privatized gains, socialized losses. Data centers consume scarce resources (electricity, water, land) and voters are starting to treat them the same way they treat factory farms or landfills.

**Bottom line:** The AI buildout can't happen without physical infrastructure, and physical infrastructure means local politics. This is going to get worse before it gets better.
