## 📦 The Stack

**[Agents] The First Fully General Computer Action Model (FDM-1)** — SI trained a computer-use foundation model on 11 million hours of screen recordings using an inverse dynamics model for annotation, sidestepping the contractor-labeling bottleneck that has kept every prior attempt tiny. The video encoder compresses ~2 hours of 30 FPS footage into 1M tokens (50x better than SOTA), unlocking long-horizon context that VLMs physically cannot reach. If it scales as claimed, this is what replaces the "screenshot → VLM → action" loop.

**[Breakout] Large-Scale Online Deanonymization with LLMs** — Researchers showed that embedding search + LLM reasoning can re-identify anonymous HN and Reddit accounts by cross-referencing inferred attributes against LinkedIn and the open web, degrading gracefully to tens of thousands of candidates. The attack pipeline is already practical; the paper (arXiv:2602.16800) includes mitigations. Your pseudonymous online presence is weaker than you think.

**[Agents] Making MCP cheaper via CLI** — Swapping MCP's upfront JSON Schema dump for lazy-loading CLIs cuts session-start token cost by 98% (15,540 → 300 tokens for 84 tools) and beats Anthropic's own Tool Search by 74–88%. Open-source converter included. Anyone running multi-server agent setups should look at this before their next invoice.

**[Architecture] Image-Video VAE: Better Reconstruction ≠ Better Generation** — Linum AI published 4 months of training logs and open-sourced their Image-Video VAE, with the key finding that reconstruction quality doesn't reliably track downstream generation quality in latent diffusion pipelines. Dense with failure modes (NaNs, co-training instability) and honest about ending up on Wan 2.1's VAE for production. Rare to see this much candor in a field note.
